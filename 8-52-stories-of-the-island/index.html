There are many dark stories about Moreton Island, not that I know or heard of all of them. But surely there is a lot to say and read about it. Some long "forgotten" of prison and camp, starvation and leprosy. Some more recent of whale hunting, great resorts and mass tourism. And the today stories of whale watching, snorkelers on the sunk boats, 4 wheel drive sand roads and big freighters in the distance... 

Despite a great will to feel all of that past, our two days on the island last week, has been only a complete idyllic adventure ! Which reminded me of Vietnam. There is something weird about getting to know ahead the story of a place or a country and to finally get there and realise it's really "history". Every piece of what it used to be, the event and the great stories are just erased, most of times for the best. How could we not be happy about the end of whale hunting and the fact that landmine are today banana fields! I still want to know though, as if I owed the place a tourist apology. 

Those photos are to show how, even as tourists, we are still looking for stories of all kind and not just trying to show the world that "we've been there"!
