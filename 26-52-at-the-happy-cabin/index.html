At the Happy Cabin

The tiny house. It is like my dream of a shed, of a shack or of a small wooden house perched in the jungle of Australia. A little handmade home that looks like a kid cabin but adult size! New Zealanders share that sense of freedom and simple life as well. And as we were moving along the country we realised tiny houses were a big matter here, some kiwis even made documentaries and web series about it!

So what is a tiny house?

That's how "Living big in a tiny house" describe it:

"Imagine living free from rent, mortgage and utility bills. Imagine living in a home that generated its own electricity and captured its own water. Imagine you could build this home yourself all for a very affordable price. Now imagine how your life would be different if you were free from debt?
The Tiny House Movement is a sweeping phenomenon in the United States, largely as a result of the recent economic troubles where many have lost their homes.
Tiny Homes are about living simply, beautifully and yet still with everything you need. Its about freedom from debt and having the economic freedom to live a bigger life, instead of having a bigger house."

It's overall a very trendy thing these days just like container homes and cafes! But it used to be just a way for people with no good amount of money to actually have a house on a land, something you could move along as nomads do, to look for work or live on the road.

Have a look at Lucy's Cedric the tiny house featured on "Living big in a tiny house" and on airbnb.
