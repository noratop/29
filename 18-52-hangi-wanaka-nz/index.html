[18/52] Hangi // Wanaka - NZ

We went to this Hangi the other day, in Wanaka and it was the first time since we've been in NZ that we felt we were doing something real. A Hangi is a way of cooking food in a hole in the ground covered with dirt (or "earth"). "Māori were aware that the earth was the giver of all life, from the soil came food and that same food was cooked beneath the earth."
Gathering around a fire with families from that small town and all eating lamb, chicken, kumara (sweet potato) and pumpkin, with our hand while some people where making giant soap bubbles, singing and watching the sun setting on Lake Wanaka and the snowy mountains in the back, felt perfect.

Being in the South Island in Winter feels a bit like you're in a giant tourist park where people try to tell you every second about the beauty of the mountains, the snow... a constant advertising for the places to go to and the ski fields and the bungy jumping, jet boating.... hell!!

I never considered that it was what I was looking for when going somewhere new. I'd rather blend in, observe and ask. Wanaka is not as touristy as Queenstown but still you are definitely in a mass tourism business.
I was happy to go to this Hangi, an event not tailored for us : the mass crowd of tourists, but a celebration of a maori culture in place for years and years. It was finally soothing.
