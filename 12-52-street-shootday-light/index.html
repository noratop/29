Street Shoot // Day light

A lot of Nora in that photoshoot, I do have only one muse!

Blogging is that great that you don't have to wait for someone to really like what you do, you can just do something productive yourself.

Australia gave me that powerful feeling that we can somehow live creatively fairly easilly.

The other day as I was skanning some blogs, one got my attention. That woman who was born in Istanbul, once working in finance in Paris, after styding in NY, moved to the US (Minneapolis), now manufacturing canvas bags made with artisan and local supplier. She said she was making them that way because she was influenced by the industrial backgroud, the environnment she was living in. That she would have done more colorful in Nepal or more crocheted in Colombia... That was actually the statement I didn't know I was looking for that day! Because when we are settling somewhere we are building instead of just living and planning the next escape. I felt inspired, here in Brisbane, all the things I was making seemed to say : "Hey it's summer, let's enjoy"! I realised we don't just make because it's how we see things, but rather we get inspired differently by each place, city and country. 

And so that came out as a big discovery!! I feel less that I'm leaving behind my Brisbane creativity but just adding it to a long list of places and inspirational ideas. Same goes with photography, each place has its own inner way of being seen. In Australia the wildlife is everything, in Asia you just want every people in those crowded streets to fit in your shots...

There's no such thing as a creative self independant of the outside world and the environnement. That being said, I can't wait to set foot in New Zealand!
