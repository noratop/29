bus / couchsurf / car hire / ferry / lift : road trip from Wanaka to Welly

  • The idea of the mini road trip popped in our minds as we were freezing in Wanaka.

  • We put that thermometer in our bedroom and were desperate to see that it was never reaching any higher than 7 degrees.

  • That's when we decided to start our way to the North, not really knowing where that would lead us!

  • We took the morning bus in Wanaka by a beautiful sunny day,

  • taking in that last view of the lake.

  • Always traveling light, we were looking at the idea of getting a car, but in the meantime we had to get to a bigger city by bus.

  • And Naked Bus: that great, cheap, kiwi company was just what we needed!

  • So, a lot of picture were taken on that bus and it always takes a few shots to get it right.

  • With the weird redish colors...

  • and I always manage to take the electric poles,

  • or blurry photos.

  • Anyway, I was like a crazy woman in that bus,

  • preventing people to sleep with my camera's "clic clic".

  • But come on, how can you sleep with so much beaut out the window.

  • (I eventually got into a little fight with Nora who was completly missing out on the views while quietly falling asleep).

  • Did I tell you how gorgeous the South Island is?

  • Look at that!

  • I did really took tones of pics of those alps mountains...

  • could'nt help it.

  • So we were slowly driving to our lunch break spot : Tekapo,

  • and our driver was such a nice guy he would stop here and there for a coffee break and for a view on a lake and so on. And we had quite a few lakes and snowy mountains on the way!

  • And then we got to Christchurch and met Paul. No photos of the guy, my apologies.

  • The sun came down as we crossed the CBD in his ute and we had a look at what the "famous" city center looks like since the earthquake hit.

  • And that's a pain to say that there is not much to describe : it is a giant construction site instead of a city.

  • Things being still demolished rather than anything being built yet.

  • A whole lot of churches are packed though and might have a second life.

  • But again, no photos to show, it was past 5pm, winter night falling down quickly. But instead, here is the most amazing : Lake Tekapo !

  • Everybody talks about that place and no wonder why.

  • Can't wait to be back in summer for some tramping and camping.

  • Lunch at Tekapo

  • Anyway, we spent the night at Paul, in his RV. (I so sound like Walter White!! // Breaking Bad reference)

  • Paul is a couchsurfer since April and get to meet people from all around the world (mostly germans and french that is!)

  • A real kiwi guy, sweet as, with an accent very very kiwi : a "Yis" and "iggs" stronger than ever.

  • Yes, kiwis do have a weird way of saying eggs and every other words with an "e".

  • We had the most interesting conversation. Talked about kiwis and how most of the people living in NZ are from somewhere else, about how kiwis wages haven't risen a notch since the 90s and why they are way poorer than Australians.

  • And how contrary to the Aussies they love to talk politics and feel concerned about everything.

  • Next morning, up at 5am and waiting for our shuttle that would get us to the airport to grab our relocation car to go get the ferry!! We said farewell to Paul

  • And we met the best shuttle driver ever. Once a tennis teacher in Holland (those kiwis travel the world so much, they feel so left out at the bottom of the world they just seek any opportunity to get the hell out).

  • eventually came back to NZ.

  • We get to the airport early!! 7am, in desperate need of a coffee.

  • We tried our first Underground coffee, a Christchurch roast!

  • Still no photo, very sorry about that, I'm definitely the worse travel photographer ever.

  • So, we get to grab "the" car.

  • Yes the proud Nora here, just loves the idea of driving that 4WD luxury to Picton 400km away.

  • Ok, so that you understand the mood here: we've been traveling for more than 2 years in cars and other means of transport of more than 20 years of age at least!

  • The oldest was from 1977,

  • and the youngest about 1997 !

  • So that brand new, shining, well working, heating fine, dolby music, beautiful car lighten our day and trip.

  • We drove and drove, seeing new lakes and mountains, barns and cute houses, long fields of whatever and not so much people anywhere.

  • The thing is most people live in the North Island and even there, like more than 80 percent of the population lives in Auckland.

  • And we stopped at Kaikoura to sea the cute seals the Lonely Planet was talking about.

  • We also had lunch : salmon bagel and fish burger.

  • (too lazy & hungry to even shoot the food or the lovely cafe).

  • But seals do make my day so I have tones of pics of those!

  • Black beach.... that what gave the name to the "All Blacks" (in the coincidence of a quizz night, you'll know!)

  • And the loveliest thing is to have the sea, the palms and the snowy mountains in the same frame.

  • Everywhere you look is a new view, that what's make NZ so incredible, all is packed on a small land of an island.

  • After staying with the seals for a good amount of time...

  • We took off to Picton,

  • to catch the ferry,

  • leave our dream car behind

  • watch a crazy sunset,

  • and feel like shit on that night ferry because of the wind and the waves (I imagine they were like 50m high, at least!)

  • On that awful boat, we got some fries and pies, very good and healthy junk food!

  • Watched Blue Jasmine and Jack and the Beanstalk (for real).

  • One after the other, real good for the headache !

  • And then we go off the boat at like 10pm and meet Rob & Cat & Lemo.

  • And Wellington!

  • So that's basically our 2 days trip to Welly,

  • with lots of driving and almost no sleep.

  • Yeah we do really enjoy road tripping rather than taking the easy plane,

  • Because we always do it the hard way.

  • It's like we love planning those impossible, tricky adventures to get to our next place!

  • And because we do have an overload of 80kg of luggage...

  • it's pretty much a nightmare to find a plane and not had 400 dol to get our stuff on board with us!

  • Anyway, we are quite happy to get a ride home from Rob & Cat.

  • 1/2 an hour later we are fast asleep in the newly finished upstairs room with Hessian coffee bags covering the walls.

  • It just feels like home.

  • The next day is a delighted discovery of Wellington,

  • the windy city and capital, and we do have quite a good "sunny" day.

  • with a light breeze!

  • It got much worse a few days later and rained and so on for 3 weeks.

  • No photos of Rob and Cat for this week...

  • but soon enough!

  • We were just too tired and lazy!

  • But here is Island Bay and a rainbow!

So overall, not a lot of photos of the key moments and lovely people we met on the way of our 2 days trip using all kind of transport and accomodation. A lot of landscapes and car window views (my dear favorites!). Better next time, right !

But that was still a nice story to tell.

I think I was too happy to leave Wanaka, to go on an adventure and to relax, to just commit to photography. So I used all my lazy tricks I've been using for a long time now, capturing while driving and being bored! But sometimes those moments you end up putting on Instagram, are also the moments you just need to enjoy to relax. So the camera stayed a lot in the bag while we were enjoying the road again.

And it was really enjoyable to be there again (even for two days) because of the amazing people we met and saw, from the first bus driver, to the second one, to the shuttle driver whose name I forgot, the 2 french girls on holiday going back to Paris, the lady from the cafe in Kaikoura, the truck drivers on the night ferry going all over NZ and to our great Welly hosts.

Not that we didn't met any nice people in Wanaka, because we did too !!!
