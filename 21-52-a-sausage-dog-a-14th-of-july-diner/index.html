[21/52] A sausage dog & a 14th of July dinner

We first planned to do some helpX (free volunteer work exchange) back when we were still in Australia. We just thought it would be the best way to discover New Zealand, the culture, the people while still being (kind of) on holidays. And it can definitely bring in some new skills: wood working, dishwashing, building stuff, sanding, cooking...! After a few weeks of that in the South Island, we just completely changed our plans! As always we don't stick to our first choices and just make up our minds in the moment! So we decided to give a last chance to that helpX thing and went to Rob and Cat's in Wellington. 

The plan was just to have a glimps at this city lavished with praise, and to be pragmatic (!) about our next move : buy a car and plan our temporary installation, rent a place, find a job, settle down for a few month and organise our next road trip around NZ.

And we were quite lucky to meet Rob and Cat! They reminded us how good it is to live the simple life, to built stuff with your own hands and to enjoy time and good food. They made us the most amazing Bastille Day dinner (14th of July) and made us feel at home. The thing is, since we left France we never celebrated or did anything for the 14th of July and it was refreshing for once to fit in a bit of tradition.
