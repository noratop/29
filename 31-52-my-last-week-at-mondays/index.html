My last week at Mondays .... potentially

Potentially

I came to realise you never know what's going to happen in that country.
Everybody is all 'sweet as bro' and all but that doesn't mean all is going well and things are settled!

So in a way, when you find a sure thing you just stick to it because there is way too much insecurity and changes in our life to keep us sane.

All that to mention that after juggling 3 to 4 jobs in the last 3 months, I sided with one and it was a quite long process. Sadly that means it might be the last time I work at Mondays, that wholefood paradise and delicious raw food kingdom where the philosophy is : Another day another chance, feel free to change.

I never considered myself a fussy eater (recently, because when I was young I was a pain in the ass my mother would say!) grabbing pretty much anything from the fridge and restaurants menus, curious of new dish from Asia, Africa and other well known lebanese and chinese... Not a vegan, a vegetarian, a paleo (no kidding!! still trying to understand why we should eat as cavemen, just imagine living like one for real, how would all those people even know what to cook without their precious internet), not gluten free, dairy free, refine sugar free... I'm just a coffee snob and that's far enough trouble for now!
So it was a pretty new thing, to work at Mondays with those food savy beautiful ladies and astonishingly enough I quite liked it and learned some pretty interesting stuff about my health.

I do feel I have to explain myself about that because I feel kind of shameful around the subject of food. I realised recently that I avoided the subject for all these years. Having relatives with food problems, I had just blocked the conversation. For years I mocked people who couldn't get their head around food, telling it was of no importance or not an interesting subject. The news, political and social issues were more decent conversation subjects. And I truly still believe it is today. I'd rather talk about how women are still subject of downsize and sexism in that country rather than have a blab about if I should put more quinoa in my salad!

BUT there are some good conversations about food and good smart people too. As when Mondays and some other places around Auckland put compost and recycling back into trend and when they sell some Spirulina produced by people in Cambodia and support their community. There are meaningful actions and talks about health and well-being and sustainability. There is just that constant talk about what you should have for dinner that oughts to stop. (in my opinion, you know it is just annoying and people don't care). But hey, it can't happen all at once!

The good thing is I feel now smarter when it comes to food and feel relatively less freaked out to even have to talk about it to people.

In all that unstable environment we live in, which we constantly criticise, and compare, we are still able to learn a lot, most of the time not even realising it. And I won't put that on the travel side of things but rather on the experiencing of new things, on changing your path and your ideas, on letting people teach you in a different way.
And that, could happen just anywhere.

A big thanks to the lovely Mondays' talented team for giving me a chance to learn even if it was kind of a harsh process! Find their website here and here.

But rest assured I'm still not becoming a vegan or stopping sugar or gluten or dairy any time soon, I'm french bon dieu!
